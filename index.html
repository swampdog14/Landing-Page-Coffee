How does Coffee Work?
Coffee cherries are picked from the tree. Coffea Arabica (The coffee plant) mostly grows in a latitude around the equator known as the "coffee belt". The two countries that supply the most coffee are Brazil and Vietnam.
The first step in coffee production is the roast. Coffee can be roasted blonde, medium and dark. The lighter the roast, the greater the caffiene, as darker roasting cooks more of the caffiene out of the bean.
There are a variety of ways to brew coffee. Most of which involve grinding the beans to a different coarseness. Pictured is an espresso machine that uses "percolation" to brew, rather than "immersion" (such as a French Press).
Coffee is enjoyed worldwide by many people. Many people make coffee at home, however enjoying coffee at a café is a great way to try new drinks, and meet new people. Caffiene-fueled conversations can be some of the most engaging.
